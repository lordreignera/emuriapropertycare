Day 1 — Account Setup Checklist

Your account has been created by the Super Admin. Below are the steps to complete before starting any work.
  • Log into https://etogo.laravel.cloud using the credentials provided to you
  • Click your name in the top-right → Settings → update your full name and email if needed
  • Change your password immediately: Settings → Change Password section → choose a strong new password
  • Upload a profile photo — click the camera icon on your avatar in Settings
  • Upload your signature image — scan or photograph your handwritten signature on white paper; upload it in the "My Signature" card. This will appear on all contracts you sign or countersign
  • Familiarise yourself with your dashboard — note which menu sections are visible to your role
  • Read this onboarding guide fully before handling your first property
⚠️ Do not skip the signature upload. Without a saved signature image, your countersignature on client contracts will show text only and no signature image in the PDF.

Handling Your First PHAR Assessment

  • 1

    Find the Awaiting Assessment List

    Go to PHAR Assessment Workflow → Awaiting Assessment. This shows all properties where the client has paid the $299 inspection fee and is waiting for the visit.

  • 2

    Open the Inspection Record

    Click the property to open the inspection record. Review the property details and any client-reported issues. Assign yourself (or another inspector) as the responsible inspector. Set the scheduled visit date.

  • 3

    Carry Out the Physical Visit

    On the visit day, take the Inspector Field Checklist (available in System Settings → Documents). Record all observations, take photographs, and note severity scores. Record one-way travel distance (km) and travel time (minutes) — this is required for pricing.

  • 4

    Fill the PHAR Assessment Form

    Back at your desk, open the inspection and click Fill PHAR Assessment Form. Work through each property system section:
    — Enter each finding: description, severity, affected area
    — Add recommended remediation tasks
    — Enter labour hours estimate and material cost for each finding
    — Upload photographs for each finding
    — Enter BDC data: travel distance (km) and travel time (min)

    You can Save as Draft at any time. The pricing dashboard at the bottom updates live as you enter data.
  • 5

    Review Pricing & Share Quotation

    Once all findings are entered, review the Live Pricing Dashboard at the bottom of the form:
    — BDC Annual = travel cost
    — FRLC = labour from findings
    — FMC = materials from findings
    — TRC = total project cost (= ARP)

    When satisfied, click Save Price then Share Quotation with Client. The client will receive a notification.

  • 6

    Approve Client's Finding Selection

    Once the client responds to the quotation (selecting their preferred findings), go to Pre-Assessed Properties and review their selection. Approve it to mark the assessment Complete.

    Assessment is now complete. The client receives their full report and the agreement becomes available for signing.

Pre-Sign Setup & Countersigning a Contract

Prerequisites before countersigning: (1) Tools must be assigned to the project, AND (2) the work visit schedule must be set. Both steps happen in Pre-Sign Setup.
  • 1

    Go to Pre-Sign Setup

    Navigate to Project Management → Pre-Sign Setup. This queue shows projects where the client has signed the agreement and paid the 50% deposit — they are waiting for Emuria to complete setup before countersigning.

  • 2

    Assign Tools

    Open the project and go to the Tool Assignment tab. Based on the approved findings (equipment, cleaning, structural, etc.), select the required tools from the catalogue and assign them. The Store Manager is notified automatically.

  • 3

    Set the Work Visit Schedule

    In the Visit Schedule tab, add each planned work visit date. Remember:
    — Visits are only Monday–Saturday, 7:00 AM–6:00 PM
    — First visit date = Project Start Date
    — Last visit date = Target Completion Date

  • 4

    Countersign

    Once tools and schedule are done, the project moves to Ready to Countersign. Open it, click Review & Countersign, read the full contract, enter your full name, tick the acknowledgment, and click Countersign.

    Your profile signature image will be embedded automatically.

    Project is now Active. Work can begin on the scheduled dates.
📋

Recording Work Logs During a Project

As fieldwork progresses, keep the system up to date with work logs after each visit.

  • 1

    Open the Active Project

    Go to Project Management → Active Projects → open the project.

  • 2

    Add a Maintenance Visit Log

    In the Work Logs section, click Add Visit Log. Enter the visit date, tasks completed for each finding, and any notes. Upload photos of completed work where applicable.

  • 3

    Progress Updates Automatically

    Project progress = (logged visit dates ÷ total scheduled visit dates) × 100. The client can see the progress bar update in real time from their dashboard.

  • 4

    Mark Project Complete

    When fieldwork is done (≥75% progress), click Mark as Complete. This closes the project, records the actual end date, and notifies the client. Remind the Store Manager to confirm tool returns.

User Management (Super Admin / Administrator)

All non-client users must be created by an Administrator. Clients self-register.

ActionWhere to GoNotes
Create a new staff accountAccess Control → User Management → New UserAssign the correct role (PM, Inspector, Store Manager, etc.)
Change a user's roleAccess Control → User Management → Edit UserRole change takes effect immediately on next login
Deactivate a userAccess Control → User Management → DeactivateUser can no longer log in; their historical data is preserved
Create / edit rolesAccess Control → Role ManagementSuper Admin only
Assign permissionsAccess Control → Permission ManagementSuper Admin only — fine-grained route-level permissions
Never share account credentials. Every staff member must have their own individual account. Shared logins make audit trails unreliable.
