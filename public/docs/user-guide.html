1. Getting Started — Accessing the System

Live Platform URL Visit https://etogo.laravel.cloud from any browser to access the EMURIA system.

Who registers themselves?

User Type How They Get Access
Client Self-registers on the login page → clicks "Register" → fills name, email, password.
Administrator Account created by the Super Admin via Access Control → User Management. Does NOT self-register.
Project Manager Account created by the Super Admin via Access Control → User Management.
Inspector Account created by the Super Admin via Access Control → User Management.
Store Manager Account created by the Super Admin via Access Control → User Management.
Technician Account created by the Super Admin via Access Control → User Management.

Logging In

  1. Go to https://etogo.laravel.cloud
  2. Enter your email address and password
  3. Click Login
  4. You are redirected to your role-appropriate dashboard automatically
⚠️ Forgot your password? Click "Forgot your password?" on the login page and follow the email reset link.

  • 1

    Register Your Account

    Visit https://etogo.laravel.cloud. On the login page click Register. Enter your full name, email address, and choose a strong password. Click Register to create your account. You will be taken directly to your Client Dashboard.

  • 2

    Add Your Property

    In the left sidebar go to 🏠 Properties → Add Property.
    Fill in your property details including the property name, address, type and size.
    Important: In the "Known Issues / Problem Areas" section, describe any existing problems you are aware of on the property. This helps our assessors prepare before their visit.
    Click Submit Property. Your property is now registered.

    After submission, you can view all your registered properties under Properties → My Properties.
  • 3

    Pay the Inspection Fee — $299 USD

    After registering your property, go to Services → Inspections.
    You will see your property listed with a "Pay Inspection Fee" button.
    The inspection fee is $299 USD (one-time per property assessment).
    Click the button and complete the Stripe payment form with your card details.
    Once paid, the status updates to Fee Paid and an Emuria administrator is notified automatically.

  • 4

    Await the Inspection Notification

    After payment, an administrator will schedule a physical inspection visit to your property.
    You will receive a notification (shown in the system under your Dashboard) with the confirmed inspection date and time.
    You do not need to do anything at this stage — simply prepare access to your property on the scheduled date.

    Inspection visit: An Emuria Inspector or Project Manager will visit your property on the scheduled date to carry out the full PHAR (Property Health Assessment Report) inspection.
  • 5

    Review Your Quotation

    After the on-site assessment is complete, the inspector prepares a detailed report and shares a quotation with you.
    You will receive a notification. Go to Services → Quotations to view it.
    The quotation lists all findings discovered during the inspection with recommended remediation tasks and pricing.

    You have two options:

    • ✅ Select All Findings — approve the full scope of recommended work
    • 🎯 Select Only Urgent Findings — choose which findings you want addressed first

    After selecting your preferred findings, click Submit Response. Emuria will review your selection and confirm the approved scope of work.

    The assessment is not marked complete until you respond to the quotation and your response is approved by Emuria staff.
  • 6

    View Your Inspection Report

    Once the assessment is complete and your findings are confirmed, go to Services → Inspections and click your inspection to view the full report.
    The report shows all approved findings, photographs, and the agreed scope of remediation work.

  • 7

    Sign the Service Agreement & Pay 50% Deposit

    After viewing your report, you will be presented with the Client Job Approval & Service Agreement.
    This agreement outlines the work scope, schedule, and payment terms.

    You must complete both of the following:

    • ✍️ Sign the Agreement — enter your full name and check the confirmation box. If you have uploaded a signature image in your Profile Settings, it will automatically appear on the contract.
    • 💳 Pay 50% deposit of the total project cost
    Work cannot begin until both steps are done. After you sign and pay, the Emuria team will assign tools, set the visit schedule, and countersign the agreement.
  • 8

    Await Etogo Countersignature & Project Kickoff

    Once you have signed and the 50% deposit is confirmed, an authorized Emuria representative will:
    assign the necessary tools and equipment to your project, finalize the work visit schedule, and then countersign the agreement.

    You will be notified when the agreement is fully executed. Work will then commence on the scheduled dates.

  • 9

    Track Your Active Project

    Go to Services → Projects Preview to monitor your project in real time.
    Each project card shows:

    • 📊 Progress bar — percentage of work completed
    • 📅 Start & End dates from the signed contract
    • 🏷️ Status badge — Pending / Active / In Progress / Completed
    • 💰 Payment summary — total cost, amount paid, and balance due
    If your project reaches 100% and you still have an outstanding balance, a yellow balance reminder will appear on your Dashboard. Settle the remaining amount to fully close the project.
  • 10

    Remaining Payments

    Depending on your payment plan (Full / Per Visit / Installment), you may have further payments due during the project.
    Go to Billing & Finance → Invoices to see all your invoices and outstanding balances.
    A Pay Now button will appear on your project card whenever a payment is due.

  • 11

    Submit a Service Request

    If you encounter a new issue or need additional support during or after a project, go to Services → Service Requests.
    Click New Request, describe the issue, and submit. Our team will triage and respond.

3. Administrator / Project Manager / Inspector Guide

Roles: Super Admin · Administrator · Project Manager · Inspector
Access: Admin and staff accounts are created by the Super Admin. You do not self-register. Log in at https://etogo.laravel.cloud using the credentials provided to you.
Login
→
Schedule Inspection
→
Fill PHAR Form
→
Share Quotation
→
Complete Assessment
→
Pre-Sign Setup
→
Countersign
→
Work Logs
  • 1

    Log Into the Admin Dashboard

    Go to https://etogo.laravel.cloud and log in with your email and password.
    You will land on the Admin Dashboard showing active projects, pending assessments, and key metrics.

  • 2

    View Properties Awaiting Inspection

    In the sidebar go to PHAR Assessment Workflow → Awaiting Assessment.
    This lists all properties where the client has registered and paid the inspection fee ($299).
    Click on a property to open its inspection record.

  • 3

    Schedule the Inspection Visit

    Inside the inspection record, set the scheduled visit date and assign the Inspector or Project Manager responsible for the visit.
    The client receives an automatic notification of the scheduled date.

  • 4

    Conduct the On-Site Visit & Fill the PHAR Assessment Form

    On the day of the visit, the Inspector or PM physically inspects the property.
    Back in the admin dashboard, open the inspection and click Fill PHAR Assessment Form.
    For each system and subsystem of the property, record:

    • 🔍 Findings — issues observed (condition, severity)
    • 📷 Images — photographs of each finding
    • 💡 Recommendations — proposed remediation tasks
    • 📐 BDC Inputs — property size, visit frequency, complexity parameters for pricing
    You can Save as Draft at any time and come back to continue filling the form before sharing anything with the client.
  • 5

    Compute Pricing & Share Quotation

    After completing the findings and BDC data entry, the system automatically computes the ARP (Annual Regenerative Program) pricing.
    Review the total cost, then click Save Price and Share Quotation with Client.
    The client is notified that their quotation is ready to review.

    Important: You cannot mark the assessment as complete until the client has reviewed the quotation and confirmed their selected findings.
  • 6

    Approve Client's Quotation Response & Complete Assessment

    Go to PHAR Assessment Workflow → Pre-Assessed Properties.
    When the client has responded to the quotation selecting their preferred findings, review their selection.
    Approve the client-selected findings. The inspection is then marked Completed and the client receives their full report and agreement.

  • 7

    Pre-Sign Setup — Assign Tools & Schedule Visits

    Once the client has signed the agreement and paid their 50% deposit, go to:
    Project Management → Pre-Sign Setup

    Here you will see properties that are ready for the setup phase. For each project you must:

    • 🔧 Assign Tools — select and assign all equipment needed based on the approved findings
    • 📅 Set Work Visit Schedule — define all planned visit dates (Mon–Sat, 7 AM–6 PM only). The first visit date becomes the Project Start Date; the last visit date becomes the Target Completion Date.
    A project cannot be countersigned until both tools are assigned AND the visit schedule is set.
  • 8

    Countersign the Agreement

    Once tools and schedule are set, go to:
    Project Management → Ready to Countersign

    Open the project and click Review & Countersign. Review the fully executed contract, enter your full name, check the acknowledgment box, and click Countersign.
    If you have uploaded your signature image in Profile Settings, it will be embedded on the contract automatically.

    After countersigning: the agreement is fully executed and the project officially moves to Active status. Work may now begin on the scheduled visit dates.

    ✅ The project is now live. Both parties have signed. The visit schedule is locked and work can commence.
  • 9

    Record Work Logs (Maintenance Visit Logs)

    As fieldwork progresses, navigate to:
    Project Management → Active Projects

    Open the project and go into Work Logs. For each visit, log:

    • 🗓️ Visit date
    • ✅ Tasks accomplished for each finding
    • 📊 Progress status per finding
    • 📝 Notes and photographs

    Progress is automatically calculated as (logged visit dates ÷ total scheduled dates) × 100.

  • 10

    Mark Project as Complete

    When the fieldwork reaches 75% or more completion and all findings are resolved, you can click Mark as Complete on the project.
    This sets the project status to Completed and records the actual end date.
    The client is notified and their project card updates to show the Completed badge.

    After completion: All assigned tools must be returned. Notify the Store Manager to confirm tool returns.

4. Store Manager Guide

Role: Store Manager
The Store Manager is responsible for tool inventory — assigning tools to projects before work starts and confirming tools have been returned when a project completes.
  • 1

    Log In & Access the Store Dashboard

    Log in at https://etogo.laravel.cloud with your credentials. Your dashboard focuses on Tool Operations — no PHAR assessment workflow is visible to you.

  • 2

    View Projects Awaiting Tool Assignment

    Go to Tool Operations → Awaiting Tool Assignment.
    This lists all projects where the client has signed and paid, but tools have not yet been assigned. The badge count tells you how many are pending.

  • 3

    Assign Tools to a Project

    Open a project from the list. Based on the approved findings and work scope, select the tools and equipment to be assigned. Enter the quantity for each tool and click Assign.
    The system deducts from available inventory and records the assignment against the project.

  • 4

    Confirm Tool Returns After Project Completion

    When a project is marked complete, go to Tool Operations → Tool Return & Assignment.
    Find the completed project, verify the tools returned in the field, and click Confirm Return for each item.
    Returned tools are added back into available inventory automatically.

  • 5

    Manage Tool Settings (Inventory Catalogue)

    Go to Tool Management → Tool Settings.
    Here you can add new tools to the catalogue, set quantities, update descriptions, and manage stock levels available for assignment.

6. Profile & Settings — All Users

All Roles

Every user — whether Client, Administrator, Inspector, Project Manager or Store Manager — can manage their personal profile from the Settings page.

How to Access Settings

Click your name or avatar in the top-right navbar and select Settings, or navigate directly to:
https://etogo.laravel.cloud/settings

What You Can Do

  • 1

    Update Profile Photo

    On the Settings page, click the camera icon over your profile picture and select a new image (JPG, PNG or WEBP, max 2 MB).
    The photo uploads and saves automatically as soon as you select it — no additional button click needed.

  • 2

    Upload Your Signature Image

    In the My Signature card on the left, click the file input and select your signature image (JPG, PNG or WEBP, max 2 MB).
    Use a clear handwritten signature on a white background for best results.
    The signature uploads automatically when selected.

    Why this matters: When you sign a service agreement or countersign a contract, your saved signature image is automatically embedded into the contract document and included in the downloadable PDF. This makes your agreements legally clear and professional.
  • 3

    Update Name & Email

    In the Profile Information section on the right, update your full name and email address and click Save Changes.

  • 4

    Change Your Password

    Scroll down to the Change Password section. Enter your current password, then your new password twice. A real-time password strength meter shows how strong your new password is (aim for Strong or Very Strong).
    Click Update Password to save.

    Password requirements: Minimum 8 characters, at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one number.
