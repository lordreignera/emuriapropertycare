A clause-by-clause explanation of the Client Job Approval & Service Agreement — what each section means and what both parties are agreeing to.

📄

What Is the Service Agreement?

The Client Job Approval & Service Agreement is the legally binding contract between the Client and EMURIA Regenerative Property Care. It is generated automatically by the system after the PHAR assessment is complete and the client's finding selection has been approved.

The agreement is digital-first: the client signs via the platform, and an authorised Emuria representative countersigns. Both signatures — including uploaded signature images — are embedded in the PDF record.

Signing Flow

Step 1 — Client Signs
  • Client reviews the full agreement online
  • Enters their full legal name
  • Checks the confirmation checkbox
  • Clicks "Sign Agreement"
  • Profile signature image is embedded automatically
→
Step 2 — Emuria Countersigns
  • Admin assigns tools and sets visit schedule
  • Opens Ready to Countersign queue
  • Enters their full legal name
  • Clicks "Countersign"
  • Profile signature image embedded automatically
After both parties have signed, the project moves to Active status and work may commence on the scheduled visit dates.

Clause-by-Clause Explanation

1
Parties to the Agreement
Identifies the two parties entering the contract:
— Client: The registered property owner as identified in the EMURIA system
— Service Provider: EMURIA Regenerative Property Care (Etogo)

Both parties are identified by their system-registered names. The agreement date is automatically set to the date of client signature.
2
Property Description
Specifies the exact property that is the subject of the agreement — including the property name, address, type, and size as registered in the system. This ensures the agreement is property-specific and cannot be applied to another property.
3
Approved Findings & Scope of Work
Lists all approved findings from the PHAR assessment — the specific defects, systems, and conditions that the remediation programme will address. This is the agreed scope of work.

Important: Only the findings selected and approved by the client during the quotation review process are included. No work outside this agreed scope will be carried out without a separate Change Order.

The scope is immutable after both parties sign — it cannot be changed without a signed amendment.
If additional issues are discovered during fieldwork, they are raised as a new Change Order and require fresh agreement from both parties before any additional work begins.
4
Annual Recurring Price (ARP) & Payment Terms
States the agreed total project price — the ARP (Annual Recurring Price) — calculated by the PHAR pricing engine as: BDC + FRLC + FMC.

Payment schedule:
PaymentAmountTiming
50% DepositARP ÷ 2Due at agreement signing — before work commences
Remaining 50%ARP ÷ 2Per the chosen payment plan (Full / Per Visit / Installment)
Work does not begin until the 50% deposit is confirmed paid in the system.
5
Work Schedule & Visit Dates
Specifies the planned work visit dates as set by the Project Manager in the Pre-Sign Setup stage.

— Project Start Date: Date of the first scheduled work visit
— Target Completion Date: Date of the last scheduled work visit
— Visits are scheduled Monday–Saturday, 7:00 AM–6:00 PM only

The client agrees to provide reasonable access to the property on scheduled visit days. Advance notice of at least 24 hours will be given for each visit.
Schedule changes after signing require notification to both parties and are updated in the system's Visit Schedule tab.
6
Service Tier & Service Level
States the client's assigned service tier — Essentials, Premium, White-Glove, or Critical Care — as determined by the dual-gate CPI/ARP assessment.

The tier defines the service intensity, response times, and quality standards that apply throughout the programme. Higher tiers involve more frequent visits, more comprehensive remediation, and stricter SLAs.
7
Client Obligations
The client agrees to:
— Provide safe access to the property on all scheduled visit days
— Ensure utilities (water, electricity) are accessible to the Emuria team
— Make payments on the agreed schedule
— Notify Emuria immediately if access conditions change or emergency issues arise
— Not instruct any third-party contractors to work on the same approved findings without notifying Emuria
8
EMURIA Obligations
EMURIA agrees to:
— Carry out all approved remediation work with reasonable skill and care
— Deploy qualified and vetted field technicians on all visits
— Maintain comprehensive work logs for every visit
— Notify the client at least 24 hours before each visit
— Provide photographic evidence of work completed
— Respond to emergency service requests within the tier SLA
9
Digital Signature & Legal Validity
Both parties agree that electronic signatures applied through the EMURIA platform constitute legally valid signatures equivalent to handwritten signatures for the purposes of this agreement.

Each signature is recorded with:
— The signatory's full printed name
— Date and time of signing (server timestamp)
— Signature image (if uploaded to the signatory's profile)
— System user account record

"Digitally Signed" text always appears on the agreement regardless of whether a signature image has been uploaded.
10
Termination & Cancellation
Either party may request termination of the agreement with written notice.

— If the client cancels after the 50% deposit is paid but before work begins, a cancellation fee may apply
— If work is partially complete at cancellation, payment is due for work actually completed, pro-rated to the percentage of approved findings addressed
— EMURIA may suspend service if payment obligations are not met after two written notices
Cancellation requests must be submitted through the system via a Change Order or by contacting your account manager directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

QuestionAnswer
Can a client sign without uploading a signature image?Yes. The "Digitally Signed" text will appear on the contract regardless. The image is optional but strongly recommended for professionalism.
Can the scope of work be changed after signing?Only via a formal Change Order that both parties must sign. The original agreement is immutable.
What if the client wants to add findings that weren't in the original assessment?A new assessment or a Change Order is required. These are treated as a separate contract amendment.
Can Emuria start work before the client pays the 50% deposit?No. The system blocks the project from being countersigned until the deposit payment is confirmed.
Where is the signed PDF stored?The PDF is generated on demand and can be downloaded from the inspection/project detail page at any time by both the client and admin.
What happens if the countersigning admin leaves the company?The PDF record preserves the signature at the time of signing. A new agreement would only be needed for new projects. Historical agreements remain legally valid.
