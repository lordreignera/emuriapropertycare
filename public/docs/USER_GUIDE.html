1 · User Roles & Account Types

The EMURIA system has two main portals: the Admin Portal (staff) and the Client Portal. Every user is assigned one role that determines what they see and can do.

Role Portal Who Creates the Account Key Responsibilities
Super Admin Super Admin Admin System seeded (pre-created) Full system control, create all staff accounts, configure pricing
Administrator Admin Admin Super Admin Manage staff, approve properties, countersign contracts
Project Manager PM Admin Super Admin / Admin Assign inspectors, create projects, schedule visits
Inspector Inspector Admin Super Admin / Admin Conduct PHAR assessments, upload findings & photos
Technician Technician Admin Super Admin / Admin Execute scheduled maintenance visits, upload after-photos, log work
Finance Finance Admin Super Admin / Admin View invoices, billing, payment reports
Client Client Client Self-registers at /register Register properties, pay inspection fee, sign agreement, pay for work

Default / Seeded Credentials

RoleEmailPasswordLogin URL
Super Admin admin@emuria.com @dm1n2@25 http://localhost/login
Client (example) client@example.com set on registration http://localhost/login
Note: All staff accounts (Admin, PM, Inspector, Technician, Finance) are created by the Super Admin through /admin/users/create. Clients self-register for free at /register.

2 · Client Registration & Login

Who performs this: Client (property owner / manager)

2.1 Registering a New Account

1
Visit the registration page Go to http://localhost/register. Registration is completely free — no credit card is needed at this stage.
2
Fill in your details Enter your full name, email address, and a secure password (minimum 8 characters). Accept the terms and conditions.
3
Verify your email An email verification link is sent to your inbox. Click the link to activate your account. Check your spam/junk folder if it does not arrive within 2 minutes.
4
Log in Go to http://localhost/login, enter your email and password. You are redirected to your Client Dashboard.

2.2 What the Client Dashboard Shows

After logging in, clients land on the Client Dashboard which shows:

  • Your registered properties and their status
  • Any active inspections linked to your properties
  • Outstanding payments or actions required
  • Quick links to view reports and sign agreements
After Login URL: http://localhost/dashboard — the system auto-detects your role and shows the correct dashboard view.

3 · Property Registration

Who performs this: Client

3.1 Registering Your Property

1
Navigate to My Properties From the left sidebar, click My Properties or go to http://localhost/client/properties/create.
2
Complete the property form Fill in:
  • Property Name – e.g., "Kiwatule Apartments Block A"
  • Address – full physical address
  • Property Type – residential, commercial, industrial
  • Number of Units (for commercial/multi-unit)
  • Building Size (sq ft)
  • Year Built
  • Description / Notes – anything you want the inspector to know
3
Upload property photos Upload up to 10 exterior and interior photos. Accepted formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP (max 5 MB each). Good photos help the inspector prepare.
4
Submit for review Click Submit Property. The property status becomes Pending Review. An admin will review it before scheduling an inspection.

4 · Inspection Fee Payment

Who performs this: Client · Amount: $299.00

After the admin approves your property and schedules an inspection, you are required to pay a one-time $299 inspection fee before the inspector visits. This fee covers the PHAR (Property Health Assessment Report) assessment.

4.1 Paying the Inspection Fee

1
Receive payment notification You will see a banner on your dashboard and inspections list prompting you to pay the inspection fee.
2
Click "Pay Inspection Fee" Go to http://localhost/client/inspections/{id}/checkout or click the "Pay Now" button on your inspection card.
3
Complete Stripe Checkout You are redirected to Stripe's secure hosted payment page. Enter your card details:
  • Card number, expiry, and CVC
  • Billing name and email
Click Pay $299.00.
4
Payment confirmed You are redirected back to the success page. Your inspection fee status changes to Paid. A receipt/invoice is available to download from the inspection detail page.

4.2 What Happens After Payment

  • The inspection is unlocked for the inspector to begin the PHAR Assessment
  • The admin receives notification that the fee is paid
  • The inspector is assigned (if not already) and their dashboard shows the property as ready
Test Card (sandbox/testing environment): Card number 4242 4242 4242 4242 · Any future expiry · Any 3-digit CVC

5 · Reading the Inspection Report

Who performs this: Client — after inspector completes the assessment

Once the inspector completes the PHAR assessment and an admin marks it as complete, you can view the full inspection report from your client portal.

5.1 Accessing the Report

1
Go to My Inspections From your dashboard sidebar, click My Inspections, or visit http://localhost/client/inspections.
2
Click "View Report" Find your inspection (status: Completed) and click the View Report button. URL: /client/inspections/{id}/report

5.2 What the Report Contains

SectionDescription
Property SummaryAddress, type, size, inspection date
Condition ScoreOverall property health score calculated by the CPI engine
FindingsAll issues found during inspection — each with priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low), photos, estimated labour hours, and material costs
Pricing BreakdownBDC (Base Delivery Cost), FRLC (Fixed Running Labour Cost), FMC (Fixed Material Cost), TRC (Total Regeneration Cost), and the final ARP (Annual Regeneration Price)
Payment PlanOption chosen: Full payment or Per-Visit plan, total visits per year
Visit ScheduleScheduled visit dates (once admin sets them after contract is signed)
Add PhotosClient can upload additional photos against any finding using the "Add Photo" button
Next Step: After reviewing the report, the client proceeds to sign the Job Approval Agreement (see Section 6).

6 · Signing the Job Approval Agreement

Who performs this: Client — Step 1 of 3 in the contract process

3-Step Signing Process: The agreement is only binding after all three steps are completed — (1) Client signs → (2) Client pays for work → (3) Etogo countersigns.

6.1 Reading the Agreement

1
Access the agreement From the inspection report page, click View & Sign Agreement. URL: /client/inspections/{id}/agreement
2
Read the full contract The agreement includes:
  • Scope of work and services included
  • ARP pricing (annual cost) and payment plan details
  • Project Timeline — planned start and estimated completion
  • Working hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM only
  • Visit schedule table (if already set by admin)
  • Terms, obligations and cancellation policy
  • Authorization section showing all 3 signing steps
3
Sign the agreement At the bottom of the agreement, enter:
  • Your full legal name
  • Check the acknowledgment checkbox confirming you have read and agree
Click Sign Agreement.
4
Download a copy After signing, click Download PDF to save a copy of the signed agreement for your records.

6.2 After Signing

  • The agreement shows your name and signature date
  • The page now prompts you to proceed to work payment (Step 2)
  • Etogo (admin) is notified and will countersign after payment (Step 3)

7 · Work Payment

Who performs this: Client — Step 2 of 3 in the contract process

After signing the agreement, the client must pay for the maintenance work before Etogo countersigns and work officially begins. Two payment options are available:

PlanHow It WorksBest For
Full Payment Pay the entire annual ARP amount in one lump sum. Calculated as TRC (Total Regeneration Cost). Clients who prefer a single transaction
Per-Visit Plan The total annual cost is divided equally by the number of scheduled visits (bdc_visits_per_year). One payment is processed per visit. Clients who prefer to spread payments across visits

7.1 Paying for Work

1
Click "Proceed to Work Payment" From the agreement page, click the payment button. URL: /client/inspections/{id}/work-payment
2
Choose your payment plan Select Full Payment or Per-Visit Plan. The page shows the amount due for each option.
3
Complete Stripe payment You are taken to Stripe's secure payment page. Enter your card details and confirm the payment.
4
Payment confirmed Your work payment status changes to Paid. The system notifies the admin to countersign.

7.2 Per-Visit — Subsequent Payments

If you selected the Per-Visit plan, subsequent payments are triggered for each upcoming visit. A "Pay for next visit" button appears on your dashboard before each scheduled visit date.

8 · Tracking Your Visit Schedule

Who performs this: Client — read-only view

Once Etogo countersigns the agreement and sets the visit schedule, you can track all scheduled maintenance visits from your inspection report page.

8.1 Where to Find the Schedule

Go to /client/inspections/{id}/report and scroll down to the Visit Schedule section. It shows:

ColumnWhat It Means
Visit #Sequential visit number (e.g., Visit 1, Visit 2…)
DateThe confirmed visit date (weekdays only, Mon–Fri)
DayDay of the week
HoursScheduled working hours (9:00 AM – 5:00 PM)
StatusScheduled / In Progress / Completed
When visits are completed: The row turns green and shows "Completed". The technician will have uploaded after-photos which can be viewed in the Maintenance Visit Log section.
Schedule Not Yet Available? If you have signed and paid but the schedule is not visible yet, it means the admin is still finalising visit dates. You will see: "Visit Schedule Pending — being finalised by the Etogo team."

10 · Inspector: PHAR Assessment Form

Who performs this: Inspector — on-site and in the admin portal

The PHAR (Property Health Assessment Report) is the core technical assessment of the property. It has two phases completed by the inspector.

10.1 Phase 1 – On-Site Findings

1
Open the assigned inspection From your Inspector Dashboard, click the property or go to /inspections → Awaiting Assessment.
2
Record findings on-site Use the Inspection Form on the show page to enter Phase 1 findings:
  • System / subsystem (e.g., Plumbing, Electrical, Roofing)
  • Issue description / task question
  • Severity: Critical · High · Medium · Low · NOI Protection
  • Upload finding photos (before photos — important: these become the BEFORE images in the maintenance log)
  • Labour hours and material cost estimate
3
Save all findings Click Save Findings. Findings are stored against the inspection and are visible to PM, Admin, and eventually the client on their report.

10.2 Phase 2 – PHAR Data Entry

4
Open the PHAR Data form Click PHAR Data tab or go to /inspections/{id}/phar-data.
5
Enter PHAR parameters Fill in all required fields:
  • Property Size (PSF) — square footage
  • Condition Score — overall condition, calculated from the findings
  • BDC Travel Details — distance (km) and time (minutes) to property, rates per km/minute
  • Labour Hourly Rate — read-only (display only, set by Admin in system config)
  • Visits per Year — auto-calculated from total labour hours ÷ 8; you cannot edit this field
  • FMC Material Band — select the material cost tier for this property type
6
Calculate & Save Click Calculate & Save PHAR Data. The system runs the BDC → MergeBridge pricing pipeline and saves:
  • BDC Annual / Monthly
  • FRLC Annual / Monthly
  • FMC Annual / Monthly
  • TRC (Total Regeneration Cost)
  • ARP (Annual Regeneration Price) — the final locked price

10.3 Preview & Complete the Assessment

7
Preview the report and agreement Before completing, click Preview Report and Preview Agreement to verify all data looks correct. These show exactly what the client will see.
8
Complete the assessment Once satisfied, click Complete Assessment. This locks all pricing values — they cannot change after this point. The client is notified that their report is ready.
Important — Pricing is Immutable After Completion: Once the assessment is marked complete, all ARP/TRC values are frozen. The client will pay exactly what was quoted.

11 · Admin: Countersigning the Agreement

Who performs this: Super Admin Admin — Step 3 of 3 in the contract process

The admin countersigns only after both the client has signed AND the client has paid for the work. This is the final step that makes the contract binding.

11.1 Prereqiusites Before Countersigning

  • Client has signed the agreement (client signature + date is visible)
  • Work payment has been confirmed (Stripe payment status = paid)

11.2 How to Countersign

1
Open the inspection in admin panel Go to /inspections/{id}. Scroll down to the Agreement Status section.
2
Review all three conditions Confirm the panel shows:
  • ✅ Client signed
  • ✅ Work payment paid
  • ⬜ Etogo countersign — pending (your action)
3
Click "Countersign as Etogo" Click the green Countersign Agreement button. The system records your name and the current timestamp as the Etogo signature. The contract is now legally binding.

11.3 What Unlocks After Countersigning

  • The Work Visit Schedule form appears on the admin inspection page
  • The client can see the signed agreement with all three steps completed
  • The maintenance visit log becomes active for this property

12 · Admin: Setting the Work Visit Schedule

Who performs this: Super Admin Admin Project Manager

After countersigning, the admin sets the exact calendar dates of each maintenance visit. The number of visits is calculated automatically from the PHAR assessment (total labour hours ÷ 8 hours per visit).

12.1 Setting Visit Dates

1
Open the inspection show page Go to /inspections/{id} and scroll to the green Work Visit Schedule card. This card only appears after countersigning.
2
Review the pre-suggested dates The form pre-populates N date-pickers with consecutive weekday suggestions starting from tomorrow. You can change any date — but all dates must be weekdays only (Monday–Friday).
3
Enter the actual agreed dates Change the dates to match the schedule you have confirmed with the client. Each visit represents one full working day (9:00 AM – 5:00 PM).
4
Save Visit Schedule Click Save Visit Schedule. The system:
  • Saves the JSON schedule to the inspection
  • Sets planned_start_date = first visit date
  • Sets target_completion_date = last visit date
  • Updates the contract with the confirmed schedule
Weekend dates are rejected. The system will show an error if any selected date falls on a Saturday or Sunday. All maintenance work is weekdays 9 AM–5 PM only.

12.2 After Setting the Schedule

  • The client can see all visit dates on their inspection report
  • The Maintenance Visit Log activates with a panel per visit
  • The signed agreement PDF now shows the confirmed visit table

13 · Maintenance Visit Log — Before & After Photos

Who performs this: Inspector Technician Project Manager Admin

The Maintenance Visit Log is where the team documents every on-site visit: what was done, how many hours, and — most importantly — before and after photos per issue/finding.

13.1 Accessing the Visit Log

1
Click "Maintenance Visit Log" in the sidebar This item appears under the Services section in the admin sidebar. URL: http://localhost/maintenance-visit-logs
2
Select a property The index page shows property cards with progress bars (total visits, completed visits, log entries). Click a property card to open its visit log.

13.2 The Visit Log Page

The show page lists every scheduled visit as a collapsible card:

  • Visit 1, Visit 2… — each with the confirmed date, day, and current status
  • Status badge: Scheduled (orange) / In Progress (blue) / Completed (green)

13.3 Logging Work Done During a Visit

3
Click "Log Work" on a visit card A form expands below the visit header. Fill in:
  • Issue / Finding — link this log entry to a specific PHAR finding (dropdown of all findings for this property). Optional — can be a general visit.
  • Visit Status — In Progress or Completed
  • Hours Worked — e.g., 4.5
  • Work Description — detailed description of what was done (required)
  • Additional Notes — materials used, follow-up observations, safety notes
  • After Photos — upload up to 10 photos of the completed work (JPEG/PNG/WebP, max 5 MB each)
4
Click "Save Log Entry" The entry is saved. If the status is Completed, the visit status in the schedule JSON is automatically updated to completed.

13.4 Before & After Photo Display

Once a log entry is saved, it displays a side-by-side comparison:

BEFORE (red border) AFTER (green border)
Photos from the original PHAR finding — uploaded by the inspector during Phase 1 assessment, or added by the client on the report page. Photos uploaded by the Technician/Inspector at the time of the maintenance visit using the Log Work form.
Tip: Always link each log entry to a specific finding when possible and upload multiple after-photos from different angles. This is your documented evidence of work completed.

14 · Signing Order & What Unlocks What

The entire workflow is gated by a sequential process. Nothing can be skipped. This table shows the exact order and what each action unlocks for the next step.

# Action Performed By Unlocks
1 Client registers and verifies email Client Access to client portal, property registration
2 Client submits property Client Admin sees property in "Not Scheduled" queue
3 Admin approves property & creates inspection Admin Inspector assignment, inspection fee payment prompt to client
4 Client pays $299 inspection fee (Stripe) Client Inspector can begin PHAR assessment form
5 Inspector completes Phase 1 findings + Phase 2 PHAR data Inspector Preview Report / Preview Agreement buttons
6 Admin / Inspector clicks "Complete Assessment" Inspector / Admin Pricing locked permanently; client report visible
7 Client views report and signs agreement (Step 1 of 3) Client Work payment button becomes active
8 Client pays for work — full or per-visit (Step 2 of 3) Client Admin countersign button becomes active
9 Admin countersigns agreement (Step 3 of 3) Admin Work schedule form appears on admin page; maintenance log activated
10 Admin sets visit schedule (weekday dates) Admin / PM Visit dates visible on client report; visit log panels created
11 Team visits property and logs work + after-photos Inspector / Technician Visit status updates to Completed; before/after visible in log

16 · Stripe Test Cards

When running in the test/sandbox environment, use the following card numbers to simulate different payment outcomes. Never use real card numbers in the test environment.

Card NumberResultUse Case
4242 4242 4242 4242 ✅ Payment succeeds Normal successful payment (inspection fee or work payment)
4000 0025 0000 3155 🔐 Requires 3D Secure Test authentication challenges
4000 0000 0000 9995 ❌ Insufficient funds Test declined payment handling
4000 0000 0000 0002 ❌ Card declined Test generic card decline

For all test cards use:

  • Expiry: any future date (e.g., 12/29)
  • CVC: any 3 digits (e.g., 123)
  • Name: any name (e.g., Test Client)
  • Billing address: any address
Live / Production: When the app is switched to live Stripe keys, real card details are used and real charges are made. Ensure you switch STRIPE_KEY and STRIPE_SECRET in the .env file before going live.
